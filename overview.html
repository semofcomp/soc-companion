The Semantics of Complementarity

Economic complementarity — the goods and services used or produced together — is encoded in the text embeddings of their descriptions, but it does not lie along the cosine-similarity axis everyone measures with. Plain cosine ranks complements below their substitutes (most severely for the strongest transformer embeddings); a one-line learned probe recovers them. The result is general: it holds across consumption and production, and across two national economies.

What this paper does

The research question

Do modern text embeddings encode economic complementarity — which products and services are used or produced together — and if so, why does standard similarity miss it, and how can it be recovered? Complements are, by definition, unalike: a flight and a hotel, lumber and nails.

What it establishes

Complementarity is present in embedding space but off the cosine axis. A simple learned probe recovers it (AUC ≈ 0.85–0.91), the finding survives every cut of the data, and it agrees with real behavioral and input–output evidence. The recovered graph then reveals hubs, ecosystems, and rare mutual pairs.

The data

≈3,000–3,500 NAPCS detailed categories per economy (Canada + United States); ≈16k–18k LLM-elicited complement pairs (~5.5 per category); same-class substitutes and random pairs as anchors; four text embeddings; four external datasets. Every headline number on this site reads from one canonical results file.

How it works

1 · Elicit
Name the complements
For each category, an LLM names the categories genuinely used together with it; same-class siblings are its substitutes.
2 · Embed
Four representations
Every category's text, embedded four ways — TF-IDF, GloVe, Gemini, OpenAI — anisotropy-corrected so each model’s cosines read against its own baselines (cross-model claims use ranks and probes, not raw cosine).
3 · Cosine fails
Complements rank low
Plain cosine ranks complements below substitutes — AUC under 0.5. Similarity is the wrong ruler.
4 · Probe recovers
A learned read-out
Logistic regression on the pair's element-wise product and absolute difference lifts AUC to ~0.85–0.91.
5 · Validate
Against the world
The recovered structure matches Amazon co-purchase, Yelp co-visitation, and StatCan / BEA input–output flows.
