External validation

The census labels are generated, not observed — so we test them against data no part of the pipeline saw, organized along the paper's axis: consumption complements against behavioral co-purchase / co-visitation, production complements against national input–output accounts. Claim A: are generated complements economically real (co-occur / link more than chance)? Claim B: does cosine still rank substitutes above complements on externally-defined pairs?

Exemplar ≠ validation source. The US is the production exemplar census, but the cleanest production validation is Canada's (StatCan I-O 2023); BEA-2017 reproduces the US production result with documented crosswalk limits. Likewise the consumption validation is strongest in Canada (Amazon + Yelp), with US Amazon concordance attenuated by coarse category mapping.
