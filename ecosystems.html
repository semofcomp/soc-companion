Complement ecosystems

Fifteen demand-side ecosystems in the complement graph

Treat the LLM complement census for Canada as a graph — 2,988 categories connected by 15,925 distinct "bought together" links — and ask which categories cluster together. Louvain community detection greedily groups nodes so that links fall inside groups far more often than chance; how well it succeeds is measured by modularity, which runs from ~0 (no community structure) toward 1. The complement graph scores 0.555 (seed 42) and splits into 15 communities.

These communities are not the supply-side taxonomy in disguise. NAPCS sections group categories by what they are (industry of origin); the ecosystems group them by what they are bought with. The average community spans members from many NAPCS sections — section entropy 2.3 bits within a community versus 2.9 bits for random sets of the same size. Lower than random (the communities are real), but far from zero: each ecosystem cuts across the official classification, mixing farm goods, manufactured products, wholesale lines, and services that demand actually joins together.
