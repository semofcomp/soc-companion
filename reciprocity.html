The 265 mutual complement pairs

Complementarity, as the LLM census expresses it, is directed: asking "what is used with a nature-park visit?" yields wedding photography, but asking about wedding photography may never return the park. Of 15,925 directed complement links, only 530 — 3.3% — are reciprocated, giving 265 unordered pairs where each category names the other. These pairs matter because cosine similarity is symmetric while generation is not: mutual pairs are the cleanest test of whether embedding geometry tracks agreed-upon, bidirectional complementarity. And they behave differently — mutual pairs sit much closer in every embedding space (Gemini cosine 0.346 vs 0.149 for one-way links) and are replicated by the independent Gemini census far more often (45.7% vs 18.7% of directed links). This page browses the Canadian census; reciprocity is rarer still in the U.S. census (324 of 18,312 directed links, 1.77%).
